Fish Derby

A national robotics competition that was held in Seattle, Washington. The objective was to autonomously collect the wooden fish cut-outs placed along two circles on an 8x8 foot track. ATtiny Swarm

A programmable and rechargeable set of robots whose functionality could be changed through the addition of an attachment 'shield'. With the abstraction of functionality into modular shields, design costs plummeted and new design possibilities emerged. By attaching the shield to one of the two base designs (line follow or obstacle avoidance), the base designs electronics were overriden and the shield's functionality took over. For example, if a Bluetooth control shield were attached to the line following base design, the robot would then be controlled via BT instead of its normal behavior of following a line.
