Life at MCC
After graduating high school, I attended classes at MCC starting in the fall of 2013. It wasn't long before I discovered their robotics competition and I decided to join. That spring, we placed 2nd in the ASEE National Robotics competition, which was held in Indianapolis, IN. The following year, I became secretary of the Engineering Leadership Council. I helped start fundraisers, host trips to neighboring universities, and helped other students through the engineering program. Again I decided to join the robotics competition and again we placed 2nd. I graduated that spring (2015) with an associates degree in Engineering Science.
